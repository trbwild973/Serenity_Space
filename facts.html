General Mental Health Statistics

  • 1 in 4 people in the world will be affected by mental or neurological disorders at some point in their lives.
  • Depression is one of the leading causes of disability worldwide.
  • Over 700,000 people die due to suicide every year, with many more attempting it.

Common Myths vs. Facts

Myth: Mental illness is a sign of weakness.
Fact: Mental health conditions are medical issues and can affect anyone, regardless of strength or character.
Myth: People with mental illnesses are dangerous.
Fact: Most people with mental illnesses are not violent and are more likely to be victims than perpetrators.

Mental Health Across Ages

  • Half of all mental health conditions start by age 14, but most cases go undetected and untreated.
  • Older adults are also at risk of mental health conditions such as depression and anxiety, often linked to physical illness or isolation.

Global Perspective

  • Low- and middle-income countries often have fewer than one mental health worker per 100,000 people.
  • Stigma and lack of awareness are major barriers to treatment worldwide.
  • World Health Organization (WHO) campaigns aim to promote mental well-being globally.
